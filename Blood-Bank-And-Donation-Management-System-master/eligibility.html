Blood donation eligibility

These are general guidelines. Local rules may vary — always check with your local blood bank.

Basic eligibility criteria

  • Age: Usually 18–65 years for whole blood donations (varies by country).
  • Weight: Typically at least 50 kg (110 lbs) to ensure donor safety.
  • Health: Generally healthy and feeling well on the day of donation.
  • Hemoglobin: Sufficient hemoglobin level (often ≥12.5 g/dL for women and ≥13.0 g/dL for men).

Temporary deferrals

  • Recent illness: Wait at least 7 days after full recovery from a minor illness.
  • Recent vaccinations or travel: Some vaccines or travel to certain regions may lead to temporary deferral.
  • Recent tattoos or piercings: Often a 3- to 6-month deferral depending on local rules.
  • Pregnancy: Deferred during pregnancy and for a period after delivery.
  • Recent donation: Wait at least 90 days between whole blood donations (varies by service).

Permanent deferrals

  • Certain chronic conditions or high-risk behaviors that could affect blood safety.
  • Some medications may permanently exclude donation (consult local guidelines).

If you are unsure, contact your local blood center for specific guidance.
